LINUS TORVALDS

The man behind Linux

Linus at the 2003 Linux Conference in Perth, Australia

A Brief History

  • 1969: Born in Helsinki, Finland to Swedish speaking minority.
  • 1981: Interest in computers defined with a Commodore VIC-20. Went on to write his own assembler and editor (even made a Pac-Man clone).
  • 1988-96: Graduated with a master's in computer science from University of Helsinki.
  • 1990: Exposed to UNIX for the first time.
  • 1991: Purchased an Intel 80386 and began work on Linux.
  • 1991: First prototypes of Linux released with a V1.0 released March 14, 1994.
  • 1991: Encountered the GNU Project with a Lars Wirzenius and ended up using the GNU General Public License (GPLv2) for his Linux kernal.
  • 1996: Went on to work at Transmeta until 2003 then on to the Open Source Development Labs which merged with Free Standards Group to become the Linux Foundation.
  • 1997: Involved in 86open to choose the standard binary format for Linux and Unix.
    • 1999: Named one of the world's top 100 innovators under age 35.
  • 1999: Red Hat and VA Linux went public and presented stock to Linus that rose to roughly US$20 million.
  • 2005: Created Git the version control system after criticism of using BitLocker a proprietary software
  • 2006 and on: Linus remains heavily involved in the open source community and retains the highest authority on incorporating new code into the standard Linux kernel.

Accolades

  • 1997: University of Helsinki has an auditorium named after Linus and his computer is on display in the Department of Computer Science
  • 1998: EFF Pioneer Award
  • 2000: Lovelace Medal from British Computer Society
  • 2001: Takeda Award shared between himself and Richard Stallman and Ken Sakamura
  • 2005: Vollum Award
  • 2008: Inducted into the Hall of Fellows of the Computer History Museum in Mountain View, California
  • 2010: C&C Prize awarded by the NEC Corporation
  • 2012: Mellennium Technology Prize
  • 2012: Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame
  • 2014: IEEE Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2018: IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award
